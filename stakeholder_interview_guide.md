# Stakeholder Interview Guide

## Purpose
To investigate critical weaknesses in current university online attendance systems through interviews with key stakeholders.

## Target Participants
- **Students** (10-15): Varied year levels, technical backgrounds
- **Lecturers** (5-8): Online teaching experience
- **IT Administrators** (3-5): LMS/Zoom integration experience
- **Academic Coordinators** (2-3): Policy oversight

## Interview Questions

### Introduction
1. Role and experience with online teaching/learning
2. Current attendance verification methods used

### Core Questions

**For Students:**
1. What challenges do you face with current attendance marking?
2. How often do technical issues affect attendance recording?
3. Have you ever been marked absent when present (false negative)?
4. Have you witnessed others marking attendance for absent peers?
5. What would make attendance verification more reliable for you?

**For Lecturers:**
1. What are the main issues with current attendance systems?
2. How much time is spent on attendance-related tasks per class?
3. What verification accuracy do you currently achieve?
4. What technical limitations exist?
5. What features would improve the process?

**For IT/Admin:**
1. What are the scalability issues with current systems?
2. How are attendance data stored and protected?
3. What integration challenges exist with LMS platforms?
4. Are there known security vulnerabilities?

## Interview Protocol
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes per interview
- **Format**: Semi-structured conversation
- **Recording**: Audio recorded (with consent) + notes
- **Compensation**: None required (academic research)

## Success Metrics
- Minimum 3 identified weaknesses
- Quantitative data on:
  - False positive/negative rates
  - Technical failure rates
  - Time investment for manual verification
